3. GRANT OF AUTHORITY

I, {{CLIENT_NAME}} ("Principal"), hereby grant to Speedy Credit Repair Inc. ("Agent") a LIMITED POWER OF ATTORNEY to act on my behalf for the following specific purposes only:

Powers Granted:

The Agent is authorized to perform the following actions on behalf of the Principal:

  1. Obtain Credit Reports: Request, receive, and review credit reports from Equifax, Experian, TransUnion, and any other consumer reporting agencies
  2. Submit Disputes: Prepare, submit, and file disputes regarding inaccurate, incomplete, unverifiable, or questionable information appearing on credit reports
  3. Communicate with Bureaus: Communicate with credit bureaus via mail, phone, fax, email, or any other means regarding credit report information
  4. Request Investigations: Request investigations and re-investigations of disputed credit report items pursuant to the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
  5. Receive Investigation Results: Receive and review responses, investigation results, and updated credit reports from credit bureaus
  6. Contact Creditors: Communicate with creditors, collection agencies, and data furnishers regarding items appearing on credit reports
  7. Request Verification: Request verification and documentation from credit bureaus and creditors supporting reported information
  8. Negotiate with Creditors: Request goodwill adjustments, payment arrangements, and pay-for-delete agreements with creditors
  9. Monitor Credit: Monitor credit reports and scores on an ongoing basis
  10. Escalate Disputes: Escalate disputes to management or regulatory authorities when necessary

4. POWERS NOT GRANTED

IMPORTANT LIMITATIONS:

This Limited Power of Attorney is STRICTLY LIMITED to credit reporting and credit repair purposes as listed in Section 3. The Agent does NOT have authority to:

  • Open New Credit: Open new credit accounts, credit cards, or lines of credit in the Principal's name
  • Apply for Loans: Apply for mortgages, auto loans, personal loans, or any other credit products
  • Access Bank Accounts: Access, withdraw from, or manage the Principal's bank accounts or financial assets
  • Make Purchases: Make purchases or payments on behalf of the Principal (except for credit repair service fees)
  • Sign Unrelated Contracts: Sign contracts or agreements unrelated to credit repair services
  • Sell or Transfer Property: Sell, transfer, mortgage, or otherwise dispose of any of the Principal's property
  • Make Financial Transactions: Conduct any financial transactions not directly related to credit repair
  • Healthcare Decisions: Make healthcare, medical, or personal care decisions
  • Legal Proceedings: Represent the Principal in legal proceedings (unless separately authorized)
  • Tax Matters: File tax returns or make tax-related decisions

5. AUTHORIZATION UNDER FCRA

Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq., the Principal hereby authorizes Speedy Credit Repair Inc. to:

  • Act as the Principal's authorized representative in all matters relating to credit reports and credit bureau communications
  • Receive credit reports, credit scores, and credit monitoring alerts on the Principal's behalf
  • Dispute inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information pursuant to FCRA § 1681i
  • Request reinvestigation of disputed items and receive investigation results
  • Communicate directly with consumer reporting agencies as the Principal's designated representative
  • Request method of verification from credit bureaus as permitted by FCRA
  • File complaints with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) on the Principal's behalf if necessary

Credit Bureau Authorization: The Principal specifically authorizes Equifax, Experian, TransUnion, and all other consumer reporting agencies to communicate directly with Speedy Credit Repair Inc. regarding the Principal's credit reports and to provide copies of credit reports and investigation results to the Agent.

7. REVOCATION RIGHTS

The Principal understands and acknowledges the right to revoke this Limited Power of Attorney at any time.

7.1 How to Revoke

To revoke this Power of Attorney, the Principal must:

  1. Provide written notice to Speedy Credit Repair Inc. via:
    • Email to {{COMPANY_EMAIL}} (subject line: "Revocation of Power of Attorney"), OR
    • Certified mail to {{COMPANY_ADDRESS}}
  2. Provide a minimum of 15 days advance notice to allow the Agent to notify all relevant parties
  3. Include in the revocation notice: full name, contract number ({{CONTRACT_NUMBER}}), and date of revocation

7.2 Effect of Revocation

Upon receipt of revocation notice, the Agent will:

  • Immediately cease all activities on the Principal's behalf
  • Notify all credit bureaus, creditors, and other parties that the Power of Attorney has been revoked within 10 business days
  • Provide written confirmation of revocation to the Principal
  • Return any original documents in the Agent's possession (if requested)

Note: Revocation of this Power of Attorney does not automatically terminate the Service Agreement. The Principal must separately cancel the Service Agreement according to its terms.
